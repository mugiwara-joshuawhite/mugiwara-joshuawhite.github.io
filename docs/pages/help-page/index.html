Financial Help Page

MyMoney

MyMoney Five & Tools
  • MyMoney is a government website run by the Financial Literacy and Education Commission
  • IMyMoney describes 5 Principles to follow in order to manage and grow your money regarding Earning, Saving & Investing, protecting your wealth, managing your spending, and borrowing.
  • Additionally, MyMoney links to various tools such as calculators for tax, retirement, investments, debts, and more. You can also find budgeting tools or checklists to follow to ensure you are financially secure.

FDIC: Money Smart

FDIC:Money Smart for Adults
  • The FDIC provides a free 14 Module, 30+ hour course providing an up to date comprehensive knowledge of managing their own personal finance.
  • The curriculum includes coverage of:
    • Your financial decisions
    • Financial services
    • Tracking income or expense, Managing and Planning your Spending and Saving
    • Credit Scores
    • Credit cards, Debt, Borrowing, and Loans
    • How to protect yourself from fraud, identity theft, etcetera.
    • Housing, Buying a home
    • Financial Planning for emergencies, disasters, and other life circumstances.

Consumer Finance

CFPB: Guides for Financial Decisions
  • This government resource provides a list of guides, resources, and tools to help you manage your finances, guides on saving and investing, planning for the future, filing tax, handling job loss.
  • Additionally, resources are provided to submit complaints regarding financial services and products if you have been a victim without compensation.

Credit Scores

FTC: Credit Score

CFPB: How do I get a free copy of my credit reports?
  • Explains what a credit score is and why it's important to maintain
  • Explains what you need to do to get a raise your credit score and what you should not do to cause it to be lower
  • Tells you how to get a credit score
  • Tells you how to get your credit report and tells you where to get it
  • Guide on how to obtain free credit reports

Taxes

IRS

U.S. Department of the Treasury:Taxes
  • These websites provide services on how to check, file, and understand your taxes.
  • Tools such as Tax calculators, forms, or other useful documents are also provided to help you prepare for tax filing.

Identity Theft and Fraud (Monitoring, protecting, and reporting)

FTC:Identity Theft
  • A list of resources provided by the FTC to protect you and your family from identity theft and fraud.
  • This resource details how to:
    • Proactively detect identity theft.
    • Protect yourself from identity theft.
    • How to handle fraud when it affects you such as by freezing your credit to limit the damage caused and filing reports.
  • This resource describes the differences between the different types of fraud, identity theft, and scams including important factors you should keep in mind with each.

Debt Relief

CFPB:What is a debt relief program and how do I know if I should use one?
  • If you have significant debt you may be able to use a Debt Relief program to have your Debt reduced through negotiation by a company.
  • This article provides various important factors about debt relief, such as risks or warning signs to avoid when searching for a debt relief service. Ensuring that you are not put in a worse situation by seeking debt relief than when you started.

Government Relief

usa.gov: Government Benefits
  • Depending on your circumstance, you may qualify for government assistance. At usa.gov you can find a list of benefits which may apply to you.
  • Relief for food assistance, utility bills, health insurance, social security, and more.
  • There is additional emergency housing services, and assistance for buyers of homes.
